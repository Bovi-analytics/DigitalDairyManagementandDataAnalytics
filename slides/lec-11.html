Repro performance “core”: 21-d pregnancy rate

On large farms, reproductive performance is often tracked with 21-day pregnancy rate, driven by:

  • AI service rate (submission)

  • P/AI (conception rate)

Your analytics should always decompose PR into IR × CR.

Timing metrics (cows)

Days in milk (DIM) to first insemination

  • Synonyms: calving-to-first-service interval

  • Definition: DIM at first service.

  • Bias - VWP policy differences; health holds - Missing early heats/services shifts later

  • Momentum - Strong pipeline; slow changes

  • Variance - Moderate; clustered by calving season/pen

  • Lag - Responds after new calving cohorts pass VWP

DIM to first heat

  • Synonyms: days to first observed estrus postpartum

  • Bias - Under-detection (short estrus duration at high production) 1 - “Observed” ≠ “occurred”

  • Momentum - Affected by transition health & energy balance

  • Variance - High without sensors

  • Lag - First weeks postpartum

Count/intensity metrics

Number of inseminations per pregnancy

  • Synonyms: services per conception, S/C

  • Definition: total services leading up to conception.

  • Bias - Censoring: open cows not yet pregnant - Mis-linked pregnancy to service

  • Momentum - Improves slowly; repeat breeders dominate

  • Variance - High; long tail distribution

  • Lag - Requires pregnancy outcome

Number of inseminations per inseminated cow (insemination number)

  • Synonyms: services per bred cow

  • Definition: average number of services among cows with ≥1 service (in window).

  • Bias - Window truncation (late services fall outside) - Open cows inflate if tracked cross-sectionally

  • Momentum - Moderate

  • Variance - High at small n

  • Lag - Low to moderate

Number of inseminations per non-pregnant cow

  • Synonyms: services per open cow

  • Definition: among currently open cows, average services received.

  • Bias - Survivorship/culling (high-service cows often leave) - Pregnancy status misclassification

  • Momentum - High inertia; management often reacts late

  • Variance - High; heavily skewed

  • Lag - Low (operational)

Animals > 3 inseminations

  • Synonyms: repeat breeders, >3 services

  • Definition: proportion or count of cows with services ≥4 and not confirmed pregnant.

  • Bias - Depends on pregnancy diagnosis timing - Culling removes worst cases

  • Momentum - Sticky (accumulates)

  • Variance - Moderate

  • Lag - Builds over months

Detection & service metrics

Heat detection rate

  • Synonyms: estrus detection rate (EDR), detection efficiency

  • Definition (common): fraction of eligible cows in heat that are detected.

  • Practical proxy: “submission rate” (see service rate) when direct heat is unobservable.

  • Bias - True heats are unobserved; sensors change sensitivity - High milk production shortens estrus duration → apparent EDR drops 1

  • Momentum - Can change quickly with labor/sensors

  • Variance - High day-to-day, lower over weeks

  • Lag - Low

Pre-Service Heat Detection Efficiency

  • Synonyms: pre-breeding detection efficiency

  • Definition: detection/submission performance before first service.

  • Bias - VWP, pen moves, health holds

  • Momentum - Moderate

  • Variance - Moderate

  • Lag - Short

Post-Service Heat Detection Efficiency

  • Synonyms: return-to-estrus detection efficiency

  • Definition: how well returns to heat are detected after an AI.

  • Bias - Pregnancy loss delays return-to-estrus distribution 1

  • Momentum - Improves with monitoring/sensors

  • Variance - High with small n

  • Lag - Medium (depends on cycle length + diagnosis schedule)

Outcome metrics (pregnancy & calving)

Total number of pregnancies

  • Definition: count of confirmed pregnancies (cross-sectional or within time window).

  • Bias - Recheck duplication or missing updates after loss

  • Momentum - Inventory-like

  • Variance - n-dependent

  • Lag - Preg-check schedule driven

Total number of calvings

  • Definition: count of calving events.

  • Bias - Stillbirth/abortion recording differences

  • Momentum - Very slow; year-long pipeline

  • Variance - Low for large herds; high for small

  • Lag - Very long

Days open

  • Synonyms: calving-to-conception interval

  • Definition: days from calving to conception (or to confirmed pregnancy date with correction).

  • Bias - If you use pregnancy confirmation date instead of conception date → overestimates - Censoring: cows not yet pregnant

  • Momentum - High inertia

  • Variance - Moderate

  • Lag - Medium-long

Interval First Service – Conception

  • Synonyms: first-service-to-conception interval

  • Definition: days between first service and service that resulted in pregnancy.

  • Bias - Censoring; pregnancy loss misclassification

  • Momentum - Moderate

  • Variance - Skewed

  • Lag - Medium

Calving interval (historic, expected, predicted, adjusted)

  • Synonyms: inter-calving interval (ICI)

    • Historic: actual previous calving-to-calving
    • Expected/Predicted: using current pregnancy due date
    • Adjusted: corrected for censored/open cows or policy (document your method)

  • Bias - Open cows missing; induced calving; pregnancy loss

  • Momentum - Very high inertia

  • Variance - Low in large herds

  • Lag - Very long

Percentage pregnant animals

  • Synonyms: pregnancy prevalence

  • Definition: pregnant / (pregnant + eligible open) at a snapshot.

  • Bias - Eligibility definition; missing pregnancy loss updates

  • Momentum - Inventory inertia

  • Variance - Lower in large herds

  • Lag - Medium

% pregnant at 100 DIM

  • Synonyms: pregnancy by 100 DIM

  • Definition: among cows reaching 100 DIM, fraction pregnant by that point (cohort-friendly).

  • Bias - Culling before 100 DIM (survivorship bias) - Missing preg checks

  • Momentum - Medium-high

  • Variance - Moderate

  • Lag - Medium

% non-pregnant at 200 DIM

  • Synonyms: open rate at 200 DIM

  • Bias - Culling before 200 DIM inflates “good” performance

  • Momentum - High

  • Variance - Moderate

  • Lag - Long

Rate/risk metrics (IR/CR/PR + NR56)

Insemination rate or risk

  • Synonyms: service rate, submission rate (SR)

  • Definition: eligible cows inseminated in a time window / eligible cows.

  • LDHM Book context: AI service rate is a key determinant of 21-d pregnancy rate.

  • Bias - Eligibility definition (VWP, health holds) - Missing services

  • Momentum - Can change quickly

  • Variance - High in small herds

  • Lag - Low

Conception rate or risk

  • Synonyms: P/AI, pregnancies per AI

  • Definition: pregnancies from AIs / total AIs (with consistent pregnancy check timing).

  • LDHM Book context: P/AI is used as fertility measure for inseminated cows.

  • Bias - Pregnancy loss after check - Non-independent observations (same cow multiple AIs) - Tech/bull confounding

  • Momentum - Medium

  • Variance - High at small n

  • Lag - At least until pregnancy diagnosis

Pregnancy rate or risk

  • Synonyms: 21-d pregnancy rate (when windowed), PR

  • Definition: eligible cows that become pregnant in window / eligible cows.

  • Book context: 21-d pregnancy rate is widely used as the primary herd metric on large farms. 1

  • Bias - Window definition + eligibility drift - Double-count pregnancies if not careful with event logic

  • Momentum - Medium

  • Variance - Can be very high in small herds

  • Lag - Medium

Non-return % 56

  • Synonyms: NR56, 56-day nonreturn rate

  • Definition: proportion of inseminated cows that do not return to estrus within 56 days (often proxy for pregnancy).

  • Bias - Silent heats, missed heats, long cycles, pregnancy loss - Strongly affected by heat detection quality

  • Momentum - Medium

  • Variance - Moderate-high

  • Lag - Fixed by definition (56 days)

Days lost decomposition

Days lost due to suboptimal Heat Detection (post insemination)

Idea: lost time between return to estrus and next service because heat wasn’t detected promptly.

Bias - Requires accurate return-to-estrus inference (hard if heats aren’t logged) - Pregnancy loss complicates return timing 1

Momentum - Can change quickly with detection tools

Variance - High

Lag - Medium

Days lost due to suboptimal Conception Rate

Idea: extra open days caused by failed conceptions (repeat AIs) versus a target CR.

Bias - Requires a counterfactual target; sensitive to censoring

Momentum - Medium

Variance - Depends on n

Lag - Medium-long

Interpretation: - The smaller n, the larger the SE (noisy rates) - For fixed n, SE is largest near p = 0.5

How to decrease SE (three levers)

  1. Increase n
    • more cows in denominator (bigger herd)
    • longer time window (aggregate weeks)
  2. Reduce clustering
    • stratify sensibly (don’t slice too thin by pen/tech/bull)
  3. Use smoothing / sequential methods
    • EWMA, CUSUM (next section)

Rule of thumb: - To halve SE, you need ~4× the sample size.

Interpreting SE in practice

Example logic (no need to memorize):

  • If CR = 35% on n = 20 AI

  • SE ≈ sqrt(0.35*0.65/20) ≈ 0.106 (~10.6 percentage points)

That means week-to-week swings are expected even if “true” CR is stable.

Small vs large herd monitoring

Large herds

  • Weekly (or even daily) rates can be stable enough for decisions
  • You can stratify (parity, pen, tech) without destroying n
  • Control charts detect small shifts earlier

Small herds

  • Weekly rates are dominated by random variation
  • “Pregnancy rate dropped from 30% to 10%” may be just n=10 vs n=10
  • You must:
    • aggregate longer (e.g., 6–12 weeks)
    • use smoothing/seq methods (EWMA/CUSUM)
    • focus on counts + timelines (who is overdue) alongside rates

Why IR/CR/PR can mislead at small n

  • PR = IR × CR: both are noisy → PR is very noisy
  • A single pregnancy (or loss) can swing the rate dramatically
  • Therefore, use:
    • confidence intervals / SE
    • rolling windows
    • sequential charts

EWMA and CUSUM for small herds

EWMA (Exponentially Weighted Moving Average)

Purpose: smooth noisy weekly rates while staying responsive.

Update: [ EWMA_t = x_t + (1-)EWMA_{t-1} ] - x_t = this week’s rate (or 0/1 outcome stream) - small (0.1–0.3) = more smoothing

Interpretation: - EWMA shows momentum (trend) better than raw rates

CUSUM (Cumulative Sum)

Purpose: detect sustained small shifts.

Binary-outcome version idea: - Compare observed outcomes to target p0 - Accumulate evidence: - add (x_t - p0) each time step - alarm when cum-sum crosses threshold

Interpretation: - CUSUM is great when n is small but you monitor continuously.

Practical setup suggestion (small herd)

  • Track CR with:
    • rolling 8-week CR
    • EWMA of weekly CR (= 0.2)
  • Track IR similarly
  • Keep an operational list:
    • overdue to first service
    • 3 services open
    • open >200 DIM

Take-home

  • Always decompose PR into submission (IR) and success (CR)
  • Rates need uncertainty (SE/CI), especially in small herds
  • EWMA/CUSUM convert noisy signals into actionable monitoring
